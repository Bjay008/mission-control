# Mission Control product voice

This file is the copy contract for every user-facing artifact. The verbatim narration in [narration-script.md](narration-script.md) is the narrative source of truth.

## Canonical product line

> Mission control keeps long-running missions moving. by coordinating AI, tools and people, with clear, explainable decisions.

Use that line exactly as written, including capitalization and punctuation. Do not substitute “connected tools,” “coordinates,” “transparent,” “workflows,” or other near-synonyms in user-facing copy.

## Story spine

1. Long-running work loses context, clarity, and momentum.
2. Mission Control coordinates AI, tools, and people around explainable decisions.
3. Every task has an owner, every handoff has a reason, and every mission has a clear next step.
4. Bible in 365 days demonstrates the system; Mission Control is the product.
5. The signature moment is an intentional pause after Quality Control because the decision belongs to a human.
6. Approval is recorded, execution resumes, and the mission keeps moving.
7. The pattern applies to any complex mission.
8. Every mission deserves mission control.

## Voice rules

- Calm, precise, and operational.
- Explain decisions instead of describing “AI magic.”
- Make ownership, reasons, and next steps visible.
- Describe a human pause as correct coordination, never as failure or friction.
- Keep “Bible in 365 days” framed as the proof of concept, not the product.
- Use sentence case for UI labels and short captions.
