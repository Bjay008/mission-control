# Vision and MVP

## Vision

Mission Control is an AI governance and continuity system that keeps long-running missions moving without losing context, accountability, or human control.

## Product promise

Give Mission Control the mission once. It will continuously show:

- where the mission stands
- whether it is healthy
- what is blocking progress
- who owns the next task
- why that executor was selected
- when human input is required
- what should happen next

## The problem

Generative AI can produce plans and artifacts, but execution remains fragmented. People must repeatedly reconstruct context, coordinate agents and external tools, detect stalled work, manage approvals, and choose the next action.

## The solution

Mission Control maintains a durable model of the mission. It plans work, routes each task according to capability and risk, pauses for human judgment where needed, records decisions, and continuously recalculates mission health and the recommended next action.

## Differentiation

Mission Control is not:

- another chatbot
- a generic task manager
- only an AI-agent framework
- a system claiming AI can do everything

Mission Control sits above agents and tools as the mission-governance layer. Agents execute; Mission Control preserves intent, makes responsibility visible, and governs whether work should proceed.

## MVP

The hackathon MVP must prove that the system can:

1. Accept a mission.
2. Break it into executable tasks.
3. Route tasks to an AI agent, connected tool, or human.
4. Display mission health.
5. Explain why each executor was selected.
6. Pause at a human approval gate.
7. Continue after approval.
8. Preserve a visible decision and activity history.

## Signature experience

A user should understand within five seconds:

**Are we on track, what is blocked, and what happens next?**
